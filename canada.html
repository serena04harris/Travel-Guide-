Explore Whistler, Canada

Discover 38 lifts and over 8,000 acres of stunning ski and snowboarding trails at the Whistler Blackcomb Ski Resort. The town of Whistler is filled with community events, restaurants and bars, and festivals. Not into nordic sports? Enjoy hiking, biking, golf in thr warmer months instead.

Must See Attractions

Whistler Blackcomb Ski Resort

Visit this world-famous ski and snowboard mountain and resort located in the heart of British Columbia, Canada.

Scandinave Spa Whistler

Want to relax after a long day of hitting the slopes but still want to enjoy outisde? Visit the Scandinave Outdoor Thermal Spa.

Peak 2 to Peak Views

If you're not a fan of skiing and snowboarding, but still want to experince this views? Check out "Peak 2 Peak", ride up the mountain in a goldala and enjoy the breathtaking mountain views.

Getting Around Whistler

Walk

All of Whistler's neighborhoods are connected by trials and the boardwalk. Explore the town and shops on a walk!

Cabs

Available 24 hours a day, the cabs are always availble to bring you from one side of the town to the other.

Bus

Services like Uber and Lyft are widely available, offering convenience for short and long trips across the city.

Whistler Transit Bus

Free transit is available on the bus. Accessible buses that also provide room for ski and snowboard equipement.

Local Climate & Best Times to Visit

New York City experiences four distinct seasons. Spring (April to June) and fall (September to November) are ideal for pleasant weather and fewer crowds. Winter offers magical holiday decorations, while summer brings outdoor festivals and events.

Important Information Before You Go

Visa Requirements

U.S. Citizens need a passport to enter Canada. International travelers may need a visa to enter Canada. Check with the Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) website for up-to-date requirements based on your nationality.

Currency

The Canadian dollar (CAD) is the local currency. Credit cards are widely accepted, but carrying some cash is useful for small purchases and tips.

Safety Tips

Whistler, Canada is generally safe, but stay vigilant in crowded areas. Keep your belongings secure and avoid isolated areas late at night.

Travel Insurance

Consider travel insurance for medical emergencies or trip cancellations. It's a good precaution when visiting any major city.
