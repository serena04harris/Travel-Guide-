Explore Costa Rica

Costa Rica is a land of unmatched natural beauty, vibrant culture, and thrilling adventures. From lush rainforests to pristine beaches, there's something for everyone!

Walk among the clouds in this magical forest brimming with unique flora and fauna.

Getting Around Costa Rica

Public Transportation

Costa Rica has an extensive and affordable public bus network connecting major cities and towns. It's the most budget-friendly way to travel, though it might take longer than other options.

Taxis

Taxis are widely available in cities and tourist areas. Look for official red taxis with yellow triangles on the side to ensure safety and fair pricing.

Rent a Vehicle

Renting a vehicle gives you the freedom to explore remote destinations at your own pace. Ensure you have a valid driver’s license and familiarize yourself with local driving laws.

Things to Know

Roads in Costa Rica can be challenging, with some unpaved and steep routes. Always check road conditions before traveling, and consider a 4x4 vehicle for rural areas.

Local Climate & Best Times to Visit

Costa Rica enjoys a tropical climate with distinct wet (May to November) and dry (December to April) seasons. The dry season is ideal for beach holidays, while the wet season is perfect for lush rainforests and fewer crowds. Plan your trip based on your interests!

Important Information Before You Go

Visa Requirements

Citizens of many countries can enter Costa Rica visa-free for up to 90 days. Check the latest visa requirements for your nationality before traveling.

Vaccines

Make sure you are up to date on routine vaccines. Hepatitis A and Typhoid vaccinations are recommended for most travelers. Proof of yellow fever vaccination may be required if traveling from certain countries.

Currency

The Costa Rican colón (CRC) is the local currency. Credit cards are widely accepted, but it's always good to carry some cash, especially in rural areas.

Travel Insurance

Consider travel insurance that covers medical emergencies and trip cancellations. It’s especially important if you plan on adventurous activities like ziplining or scuba diving.
